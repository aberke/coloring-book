Shapes & Symmetries

INTRODUCTION




Symmetry presents itself in nature, with repetitions that can be found in natureâ€™s forms and patterns, but often with imperfections.

Math creates a space where perfect symmetry can be considered.

In our real physical world, lines may not be perfectly straight, and squares may not be perfectly square, but mathematics allows us to believe in straight lines and perfect squares.

Throughout this book, we will pretend we are in that mathematical space. We will ignore the imperfections in our drawings, and see shapes and patterns as if they are composed of perfect lines and curves. We will play with our shapes and patterns, using color to manipulate their symmetries, and even destroy them at times, all in order to better understand them.
